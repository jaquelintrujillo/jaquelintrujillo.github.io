Importance

We must find ways of reducing this problem. Asthma is a disease that has a high prevalence these days. The U.S. population spends billions of dollars each year for medicine and treatment. Different types of treatment include an inhaler, steroids, and an anti-inflammatory. The cost of medicine can go up to $100, even higher in more serious cases. Asthma is also much more common in colored populations and lower income communities. This is because these areas have poor housing qualities that trigger asthma, especially in children. Asthma also seems to be caused more by environmental factors than genetics. When an environmental crisis does occur, it causes people with asthma have more extreme symptoms. If the hazard is too dangerous, those families may even have to move.

TREATMENT

Bronchodilator/inhaler:
Opens up lungs for easier breathing.
Steroids:
This modifies hormones to reduce the inflammation.
Anti-inflammatory:
This prevents swelling in tissue.
Nebulizers:
This is an effective way to deliver asthma medications to infants, small children and to anyone who has difficulty using an asthma inhaler. It is also used when someone needs a big dose of inhaled medication. Some people need this form of medication during the wild fires. Nebulized therapy is, also, called a "breathing treatment."
