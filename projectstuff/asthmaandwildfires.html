Asthma Importance

Asthma is a disease that has a high prevalence, today. The U.S. population spends billions of dollars each year for medicine and treatment. Different types of treatment include an inhaler, steroids, and an anti-inflammatory. The cost of medicine can go up to $100, even higher in more serious cases. Asthma is, also, much more common in communities of low income people of color. This is because the areas they live in have poor housing qualities that trigger asthma, especially in children. Black children are proven to be twice as much more likely to have asthma than white children. Education seems to impact the probability of asthma. Those who didn’t finish high school have a higher probability of having asthma than those who have a finished education. Asthma is caused by environmental factors more than genetics. When an environmental crisis occurs, it causes people's symptoms to worsen. In extreme cases familes might have to move, if the environment is too hazardous. Since asthma is common, the total amount of money spent for medicine and treatment goes up to $56 billion per year. Asthma continues to increase, making the cost increase as well. Over the past decade people have a 15% more likely chance to have asthma. This both effects the school and work environment. In 2009, 10.5 million children in the U.S. missed school that year. Concerning work, 14.2 adults didn’t come in due to asthma. The struggle is there isn’t a way to cure asthma, only medication to help reduce the symptoms. As asthma keeps becoming more frequent, the problem continues to increase.

Wildfire Importance

The California wildfires have been very significant throughout the last few years. About 90% of the wildfires have been caused by humans.These fires destroyed a new state record of 459,123 acres burned last July during the Mendocino Complex Fire. The Mendocino Complex Fire was the largest fire in California history which took 4 months to put out. Not only are thousands of acres being burned but also many lives are being lost. During the Camp Fire in Butte County, California, there were 88 lives lost along with the thousands of acres burned. Also, during the Woolsey Fire in Malibu, 58 people were killed. Due to the amount of money being used to repair utilities after the fires, the State Senate passed a bill with a multibillion-dollar utility fund to pay for the distruction. These fires have impacted California and it’s residents in multiple ways and continue to be a rising problem.

When wildfires happen people:

  • Asthma people have to check in with doctor to change medicine
  • Watch out for symptoms
  • Might have to leave area for safety
